Donate

Yes, I want to help!

This is what your gift can do:

My gift of $200 or more can provide emergency housing for a minimum of 20 days.

My gift of $150 can help to provide nutritious meals for 25 homeless men and women with a mental illness.

My gift of $75 can help provide warm clothing and transportation access during the winter months for 8 homeless men and women.

My gift of $50 can help provide job readiness and relapse prevention services for individuals with mental illness.

My gift of $25 can help provide household items for a formerly homeless person placed in permanent housing.

Why Donate?

Personal wealth is not a requirement for philanthropy and you don't need a family foundation or to give so much that you receive fantastic tax credits to make a big difference. In fact, did you know that individuals make up 75 percent of giving in America? Did you also know that the wealthiest 10 percent of Americans account for only 25 percent of all charitable giving? And while large donations are important, what about little donations? Have you ever considered not giving because you don't think that your "small" donation will make a difference? If so, think again. Because in a word, YES. It will.
No matter how big or small, CFI needs your help.
Small donations add up.
It's not the size of the contribution that matters; what matters is the outcome your giving produces.
Regardless of the amount you are able to contribute, your heart is what really counts - and your willingness to give.
Not only will you make a difference in the lives of others, your life will be changed.

20 to 25% of Atlanta's homeless population lives with a severe mental illness.

Your gift can help individuals escape from unsafe, unhealthy living conditions.

You can help individuals break the cycle of poverty and build long-term financial security

Your gift will help improve the health, quality of life and strengthen community ties for men and women with mental illnesses
