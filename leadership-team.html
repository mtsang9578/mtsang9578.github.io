ABOUT OUR LEADERSHIP

Ms. Jean Toole is the President/CEO of Community Friendship, Inc. (CFI), a comprehensive provider of recovery oriented services for adults with mental illnesses in the metropolitan Atlanta. A Savannah native, Ms. Toole’s career in mental health began in 1974 at the Comprehensive Mental Health Center in Savannah. After leaving Savannah, Ms. Toole served as Director of Beacon House in Gwinnett County for two (2) years before going to CFI in 1981 where she was the Director of the Day Program/Work Opportunities Program prior to assuming the role of the executive director (now CEO) in 1984. In 1983, CFI was selected by the Center for Psychiatric Rehabilitation at Boston University as one of three sites from 40 applicants around the country to participate in intensive training in the model of psychiatric rehabilitation. Jean was one of the three (3) staff who received the training and subsequently trained CFI staff in the core skills of the psychiatric rehabilitation approach. Jean has also provided statewide training on a variety of topics related to rehabilitation and recovery. Jean has served on the National United State Psychiatric Rehabilitation Board (now the Psychiatric Rehabilitation Association) Board of Directors and is currently a Board Member of the Georgia Chapter of PRA where she serves as Treasurer. In addition, Jean received the 2014 Irv Rutman Award from the Psychiatric Rehabilitation Association for her leadership in the field of psychiatric rehabilitation. Jean is a founding member of the Georgia Recovery Initiative (GRI) and received the Communications & Leadership Award from District 44 Toastmasters in 2014. Jean was the 2012 recipient of the Georgia Mental Health Consumer Network Consumer Supporter of the Year Award. Jean also serves on the Behavioral Health Planning and Advisory Council and serves on a variety of state behavioral health workgroups. Jean has a Master’s Degree in Mental Health and Human Services Administration and is a Certified Psychiatric Rehabilitation Practitioner (CPRP).

The CFI Leadership Team is made up of a diverse group of professionals with a wide range of expertise in nonprofit management, fund development, program development and evaluation, financial management, employee relations and more. Together, we provide responsive programs and services of the highest quality to the Atlanta community. Proficient in higher educational standards, CFI’s Leadership Team combined represents over a century of expertise, education, knowledge and skills as they provide services that make recovery possible for individuals living with mental illnesses.
